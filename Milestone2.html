Datasets Description¶

This data was obtained from a comprehensive collection of hotel bookings, spanning across three years from Kaggle, Those datasets showcast the intricate dynamics of room demand for two distinct types of hotels.This information talks about datasets that encompass detailed records on room reservations for a "City Hotel" located in the bustling city of Lisbon, and a "Resort Hotel" situated in the serene vacation destination of the Algarve. The datasets meticulously record various aspects of hotel bookings over the years 2015, 2016, and 2017, including but not limited to the date the reservation was made, the demographic details of the guests (number of adults, children, and/or babies), the duration of their stay (both weekend and weekday nights), and the requirement for car parking spaces.

The first place the data came from was Hotel Booking Demand Datasets (Antonio, 2019). This dataset includes all reservations for trips that are set to happen between July 1, 2015, and August 31, 2017. It includes both confirmed and canceled appointments. The variables are described as follows:

  • hotel: H1 = Resort Hotel / H2 = City Hotel
  • is_canceled: Booking was canceled (1) or not (0)
  • lead_time: Days between the booking entry date and the arrival date
  • arrival_date_year: Arrival date's year
  • arrival_date_month: Arrival date's month
  • arrival_date_week_number: Week number of the year for the arrival date
  • arrival_date_day_of_month: Day of the arrival date
  • stays_in_weekend_nights: Number of weekend nights guest stayed
  • stays_in_week_nights: Number of weeknights guest stayed
  • adults: Number of adults
  • children: Number of children
  • babies: Number of babies
  • meal: Meal type
  • country: Country of origin
  • market_segment: Market segment designation
  • distribution_channel: Booking distribution channel
  • is_repeated_guest: Repeated guest (1) or not (0)
  • previous_cancellations: Previous bookings that were canceled count
  • previous_bookings_not_canceled: Previous bookings not canceled count
  • reserved_room_type: Room type code reserved
  • assigned_room_type: Room type code assigned
  • booking_changes: Number of booking changes
  • deposit_type: No Deposit, Non-Refund, Refundable
  • agent: ID of the travel agency
  • company: ID of the company
  • days_in_waiting_list: Days that booking was on the waiting list
  • customer_type: Contract, Group, Transient, Transient-party
  • adr: Average Daily Rate
  • required_car_parking_spaces: Car parking spaces requested by the customer
  • total_of_special_requests: Special requests made by the customer
  • reservation_status: Canceled, Check-Out, No-Show Status
  • reservation_status_date: Date of the last status update

There are 32 columns and 119210 rows in the data. Since it comes from real hotel data, any information that could be used to find out who the customer or hotel is has been taken out.

Business Questions¶

  1. Analysis of Booking and Cancellation Trends: What are the volume and patterns of bookings and cancellations across different times and conditions? This question seeks to understand the dynamics of hotel bookings, including seasonal variations, the impact of external events, and the effectiveness of marketing and pricing strategies in influencing booking behaviors.

  2. Monthly Booking Distribution: How do booking rates vary by month, and what can this tell us about demand fluctuations throughout the year? Identifying busy and slack periods can help in resource allocation, staffing, and promotional activities.

  3. Impact of Guest Origin on Cancellation Rates: Is there a correlation between the guests' countries of origin and their likelihood to cancel bookings? Insights into geographic market sensitivities can inform targeted marketing strategies and risk management practices.

  4. Customer Segmentation by Booking Behavior: How can guests be segmented based on their booking habits, and how can this segmentation inform personalized marketing and service provision? Understanding different customer profiles can enhance guest experiences and loyalty.

  5. Cancellation Rates among New vs. Returning Guests: How do cancellation behaviors differ between new and returning guests, and what does this indicate about guest satisfaction and loyalty? This analysis can guide strategies to improve retention and reduce cancellations.

  6. Influence of Deposit Methods on Cancellation Rates: Do different deposit policies affect cancellation rates, and how can this knowledge shape booking and pricing policies to minimize revenue losses? Understanding the financial implications of deposit methods can lead to more effective pricing strategies.

  7. Booking and Cancellation Rates by Room Type: Which room types are most frequently booked or cancelled, and how can this information guide inventory management and promotional efforts? Insights into preferences for room types can optimize revenue management.

  8. Predictive Modeling of Booking Cancellations: Can a predictive model accurately forecast the likelihood of a booking being canceled, and how can such a model support strategic decisions in pricing, inventory management, and customer engagement? Developing a reliable predictive model can be a game-changer in anticipating and mitigating cancellations.

These questions underscore the business imperative to harness data analytics for strategic advantage, aiming to enhance operational efficiency, customer satisfaction, and financial performance in the highly competitive hospitality industry.

It can be easily seen that the company, agent, country and children columns contain missing value. The following steps are taken to address missing values in the dataset:

    1. Drop Rows where there are no adults, babies, or children: In this step, rows where there are no adults, babies, or children are removed. These rows need to be removed because they show reservations for people who aren't going to be there, which is likely a mistake or an anomaly.
    1. Replace missing agent and company IDs with 0: If a "agent" or "company" column value is missing, this way will fill it in with 0. This step assumes that the lack of an agent or company ID means the person made the reservation themselves, without the help of an agent or company. It's easy to figure out how much the agent and company affected the bookings when missing values are filled in with 0.
    1. Fill in missing country values with the mode: Since the "country" column has category data, it fills in missing values with the mode, which is the column's most common value. People use this method instead of the mean or median because it keeps the overall distribution of the data and is less likely to add bias.
    1. Fill in missing numbers for children with the rounded mean: The rounded mean value is used to fill in the blanks in the "children" column. The mean is a good choice for filling in missing numbers in continuous numerical data because it keeps the distribution's central tendency. The number that was imputed is in line with the integer nature of the "children" column because the mean was rounded.

This is paradoxical as a non-refundable deposit should discourage cancellations. No-deposit bookings have a 0.28 (28%) cancellation rate. Lack of upfront financial commitment may not increase cancellation rates compared to non-refundable deposits.
